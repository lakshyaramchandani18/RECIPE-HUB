Servings

4

Prep Time

30 min

Cook Time

1 hr
  • Use good quality basmati rice: Rinse and soak the rice before cooking for long, separate grains.
  • Layer the rice and masala: Proper layering (rice and biryani base) helps distribute flavors throughout.
  • Let it rest: After cooking, let the biryani sit for a few minutes before serving to help flavors develop.
Biryani

INGREDIENTS


2 tomatoes, chopped

1/2 cup yogurt

2 cups basmati rice

500g chicken (or vegetables for veg biryani), cut into pieces

2 large onions, sliced

2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped

1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped

4 tbsp biryani masala

1/2 tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp red chili powder

1 cinnamon stick

2-3 cloves

2-3 cardamom pods

1 bay leaf

1/2 cup oil/ghee

Salt to taste

PROCEDURE


Preparing the Rice:

1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.

2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt and whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, bay leaf).

3. Add the soaked rice and cook until it's about 70% done. Drain the rice and set aside.


Preparing the Biryani Base:

1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions for garnishing.

2. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

3. Add chopped tomatoes, biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.

4. Add the chicken pieces (or vegetables) and cook until they are tender. Stir in yogurt, chopped coriander, and mint leaves. Cook for another 5 minutes.


Layering the Biryani:

1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer half of the cooked biryani base.

2. Spread half of the cooked rice over the biryani base.

3. Repeat the layers with the remaining biryani base and rice.

4. Garnish the top layer with the reserved fried onions, some fresh coriander, and mint leaves.

5. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the edges with dough to trap the steam.

6. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together (this process is called 'dum').


Serving:

1. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork before serving.

2. Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) and salad on the side.
