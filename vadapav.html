PROCEDURE


Making the Batata Vada:

1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves and saut√© for a few seconds.

2. Add green chilli garlic paste and saut√© until the raw smell disappears.

3. Add turmeric powder and degi red chilli powder. Mix well.

4. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes. Mix everything thoroughly.

5. Add chopped coriander leaves, salt to taste, and a little water if needed. Cook for a few minutes until the mixture comes together. Remove from heat and let it cool.


Preparing the Garlic Chutney:

1. In a blender, combine grated coconut, garlic cloves, dry red chillies, and a pinch of asafoetida. Blend to a coarse paste. Set aside.


Preparing the Batter:

1. In a bowl, mix gram flour, salt, baking soda, degi red chilli powder, sugar, and oil.

2. Gradually add water to make a smooth, thick batter. It should be able to coat the back of a spoon.


Assembling and Frying the Vadas:

1. Divide the potato mixture into equal portions and shape them into round balls.

2. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.

3. Dip each potato ball into the gram flour batter, ensuring it is fully coated.

4. Carefully drop the coated balls into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy on all sides.

5. Remove the vadas from the oil and drain on paper towels.


Serving:

1. Slice pav buns in half and lightly toast them if desired.

2. Spread a generous amount of garlic chutney on the inner sides of the pav buns.

3. Place a hot batata vada between the pav buns.

4. Serve immediately with extra chutney or fried green chilies on the side.
