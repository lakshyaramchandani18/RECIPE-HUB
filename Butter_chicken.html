Butter Chicken

INGREDIENTS

For the Gravy

4 large Tomatoes, cut into half

2-3 large Onions, sliced

3-4 Garlic pods

1 inch-Ginger, sliced

1 tbsp Degi Mirch

5-6 Cloves

1 inch-Cinnamon Stick

3 Bay Leaves

5-6 Black Peppercorns

2 Green Cardamoms

2 tbsp Butter


For Butter Chicken

2 tbsp Butter

1 tbsp Red Chilli powder

1 tsp Coriander powder


Prepared Gravy

3 tbsp Fresh Cream

1 tsp Honey

Cooked Tandoori Chicken, shredded

1-2 drops Kewra Water

1 tbsp Dried Fenugreek Leaves, toasted & crushed

Burnt Charcoal

1 tsp Ghee

Fresh Cream

Coriander Sprig

PROCEDURE


Making the Dough:

1. To make the gravy, blend the tomatoes, onions, garlic, and ginger into a smooth puree.

2. Heat butter in a pan and add the whole spices (cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves, peppercorns, cardamoms). Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.

3. Add the tomato-onion puree to the pan and cook on medium heat until the oil separates from the gravy. This may take around 15-20 minutes.

4. Add degi mirch, salt, and cook for another 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool. Once cooled, blend the gravy again for a smoother texture.


Preparing the Butter Chicken:

1. In a pan, melt butter and add red chili powder and coriander powder. Saut√© for a few seconds.

2. Add the prepared gravy to the pan and mix well. Cook for 5-7 minutes on medium heat.

3. Stir in fresh cream and honey. Add the shredded tandoori chicken pieces and mix well. Cook for another 5 minutes.

4. Add kewra water and crushed fenugreek leaves. Mix well and remove from heat.


Final Touch - Smoky Flavor:

1. To add a smoky flavor, heat a small piece of charcoal until red hot.

2. Place the hot charcoal in a small bowl and place it in the center of the butter chicken dish.

3. Drizzle ghee over the hot charcoal and immediately cover the dish with a lid to trap the smoke. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes.

4. Remove the charcoal and bowl. Garnish the butter chicken with a swirl of fresh cream and coriander sprig before serving.
